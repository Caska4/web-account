Cash on Hand

More Details

Cash on Hand is the amount of physical currency you currently hold. It is important for daily operations and emergency expenses.

Keep track of how much cash is available to avoid shortages or overstock.

Cash Out

More Details

Cash Out refers to money spent from your accounts or cash reserves.

Monitoring cash outflows helps in budgeting and understanding spending patterns.

Account Payable

More Details

Accounts Payable represents amounts your business owes to suppliers or vendors.

Managing payables is crucial to maintain good business relationships and credit standing.

Account Receive

More Details

Accounts Receive is money owed to your business by customers or clients.

Tracking receivables ensures timely collection and cash flow stability.

Balance

More Details

Your Balance shows your net cash position after accounting for inflows and outflows.

A positive balance indicates good liquidity; negative balance requires attention.
